In love with doing

My name is Gabriele.
I study Mathematical and Computing Sciences for AI at Bocconi University, serve as the captain for the varsity rowing team and work as a software engineer at Bending Spoons.

Something about me

I spent the first 18 years of my life on Lake Iseo, an alpine lake in northern Italy, mainly rowing and finding creative excuses not to listen in class.

In elementary school, my excuse was crafting origami under my desk for the school-wide business I had started to amass my currency: coloured paper sheets. Once I had a drawer with more paper than I could use in a lifetime, I figured it was time to find a better excuse.

In high school, I started training for Math Olympiad and taking rowing seriously. I won a Bronze medal at the national math finals, and a Gold at the Italian rowing championships, Incidentally, I tried debating competitions as well, won a regional Silver, and realised what everyone around me knew: I like talking a lot. Thus I opened a podcast that totalised almost three million views and stepped down into Student Government politics, winning the elections and serving for two fun years.

Excited about all I was doing, I applied to the best colleges in the US. I failed. After one year at Bocconi, I managed to get to the States anyway. I spent my freshman summer in NYC working at a startup, one of my life's most enriching experiences.

Now I'm back in Milan, still juggling being a student, an athlete, and an engineer at the same time.

P.S.: for the sake of truth, which I care about deeply, what you read here is the good part; the rest doesn't look as good on a website.
