In love with doing

My name is Gabriele.
I study Mathematical and Computing Sciences for AI at Bocconi University, serve as the captain for the varsity rowing team and work as a software engineer at Bending Spoons.

Something about me

I spent the first 18 years of my life on Lake Iseo, an alpine lake in northern Italy, mainly rowing and finding creative excuses not to listen in class.

In elementary school, my excuse was crafting origami under my desk for the school-wide business I had started to amass my currency: coloured paper sheets. Once I had a drawer with more paper than I could use in a lifetime, I figured it was time to find a better excuse.

That's why, in high school, I started training for Math Olympiad and taking rowing seriously. Fast forward four years, and I had won a Bronze medal at the national math finals, a Gold at the Italian rowing championships and was about to do a solid job in debate competitions as well.
Debating, I realised what everyone around me knew: I like talking a lot. Thus (...why not?) I opened a podcast, ran for Student Government elections, won them and served for two fun years.

Excited about all I was doing, I applied to the best colleges in the US, and I failed.
But who cares? After one year at Bocconi I ended up in the States anyway: spending my freshman summer in NYC while working at a startup was one of the most enriching experiences of my life.

Now I'm back in Milan, still juggling being a student, an athlete, and an engineer at the same time.

P.S.: for the sake of truth, which I care about deeply, what you read here is the good part; the rest doesn't look as good on a webiste.
